PCOS üéÄ
Polycystic Ovary Syndrome
‚ö†Ô∏è
Symptoms
  • Irregular or missed periods
  • Excessive hair growth on face, chest, and back
  • Weight gain or difficulty losing weight
  • Acne and oily skin
  • Thinning hair or hair loss
  • Darkening of skin
  • Difficulty getting pregnant
üîç
Causes
  • Excess insulin production causing high androgen levels
  • Hormonal imbalance affecting ovulation
  • Genetic factors and family history
  • Low-grade inflammation in the body
  • Insulin resistance affecting metabolism
üíä
Treatment Plan
  • Birth control pills to regulate periods
  • Metformin for insulin resistance
  • Anti-androgen medications for hair growth
  • Fertility medications if trying to conceive
  • Regular gynecological check-ups
  • Blood sugar monitoring
üõ°Ô∏è
Prevention Steps
  • Maintain healthy weight through diet and exercise
  • Follow a balanced diet low in refined carbohydrates
  • Regular physical activity (30 minutes daily)
  • Manage stress through yoga and meditation
  • Get adequate sleep (7-8 hours)
  • Regular health screenings
  • Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol

Frequently Asked Questions
üîç
‚ùî What is a normal menstrual cycle?
A normal menstrual cycle lasts 21-35 days, with bleeding lasting 2-7 days. The cycle is counted from the first day of one period to the first day of the next. Variations are common and can be affected by stress, diet, and health conditions.
‚ùî How do I know if my period is irregular?
Your period is considered irregular if: cycles are shorter than 21 days or longer than 35 days, you miss periods, bleeding is very heavy or very light, or periods last longer than 7 days. Track your cycle for 3 months to identify patterns.
‚ùî When should I get a Pap smear?
Women should start Pap smear screening at age 21. From ages 21-29, get tested every 3 years. Ages 30-65 can get tested every 3 years with Pap smear alone, or every 5 years with HPV co-testing. Discuss your specific needs with your doctor.
‚ùî What are signs of PCOS?
Common signs include irregular periods, excessive hair growth (face, chest, back), acne, weight gain, difficulty losing weight, thinning hair, darkened skin patches, and fertility issues. If you experience multiple symptoms, consult a gynecologist.
‚ùî How can I prevent UTIs?
Drink plenty of water, urinate frequently (don't hold it), wipe front to back, urinate after intercourse, avoid irritating feminine products, wear breathable cotton underwear, and maintain good hygiene.
‚ùî What vaccines do adult women need?
Adult women should get: Tdap booster every 10 years, annual flu vaccine, HPV vaccine (if not previously vaccinated), and after age 50, shingles vaccine. Additional vaccines may be needed based on health conditions and lifestyle.

Health Tip
