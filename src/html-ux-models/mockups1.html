Frequently Asked Questions
üîç
‚ùì What is a normal menstrual cycle?
A normal menstrual cycle lasts 21-35 days, with bleeding lasting 2-7 days. The cycle is counted from the first day of one period to the first day of the next. Variations are common and can be affected by stress, diet, and health conditions.
‚ùì How do I know if my period is irregular?
Your period is considered irregular if: cycles are shorter than 21 days or longer than 35 days, you miss periods, bleeding is very heavy or very light, or periods last longer than 7 days. Track your cycle for 3 months to identify patterns.
‚ùì What are signs of a UTI (urinary tract infection)?
Common UTI symptoms include: burning sensation during urination, frequent urge to urinate, cloudy or strong-smelling urine, pelvic pain, and sometimes fever. Drink plenty of water and see a doctor for antibiotics if symptoms persist.
‚ùì How can I manage PCOS symptoms naturally?
Natural management includes: maintaining healthy weight through diet and exercise, eating low-glycemic foods, reducing stress, getting adequate sleep, and staying physically active. However, always consult a doctor for proper diagnosis and treatment plan.
‚ùì What is the difference between PMS and PMDD?
PMS (Premenstrual Syndrome) causes mild to moderate symptoms like mood changes, bloating, and fatigue. PMDD (Premenstrual Dysphoric Disorder) is more severe, causing intense mood swings, depression, and anxiety that interfere with daily life. PMDD requires medical treatment.
‚ùì How often should I check my breast health?
Perform monthly breast self-examinations a few days after your period ends. Look for lumps, changes in size or shape, skin changes, or nipple discharge. Women over 40 should get annual mammograms, or earlier if there's family history.
‚ùì What are the warning signs of endometriosis?
Key signs include: painful periods, chronic pelvic pain, pain during intercourse, painful bowel movements or urination during periods, excessive bleeding, and infertility. Early diagnosis and treatment can help manage symptoms effectively.

When to Visit a Gynecologist
üè• Regular Screening is Important
Regular gynecological check-ups help detect potential health issues early. Don't wait for symptoms to appear.
Annual Well-Woman Exam
Every woman should have an annual check-up including pelvic exam, breast exam, and health discussion.
Starting Age: 13-15 years
Pap Smear (Cervical Cancer Screening)
Tests for cervical cancer and pre-cancerous changes. Essential for early detection.
Age: 21-65 years, Every 3 years
HPV Testing
Human Papillomavirus test, often done with Pap smear for women over 30.
Age: 30+ years, Every 5 years
STI/STD Screening
Testing for sexually transmitted infections if sexually active, changing partners, or planning pregnancy.
As recommended by doctor
Breast Examination
Clinical breast exam by healthcare provider to check for lumps or abnormalities.
Annual from age 20, Monthly self-exam
Mammogram
X-ray screening for breast cancer detection. Earlier if family history exists.
Age: 40+ years, Every 1-2 years
üö®
Visit Immediately If You Experience:
  • Unusual vaginal bleeding or spotting between periods
  • Severe menstrual cramps or pelvic pain
  • Abnormal vaginal discharge with odor or color changes
  • Pain during sexual intercourse
  • Missed periods (if sexually active) or irregular cycles
  • Breast lumps or nipple discharge
  • Severe PMS symptoms affecting daily life
  • Symptoms of infection (itching, burning, fever)
  • Planning pregnancy or contraception consultation
üíö Prevention is Better Than Cure
Regular screenings can detect problems early when they're most treatable. Don't skip your annual check-ups even if you feel healthy.
